## Top flags requiring author attention before drafting

1. **TIKTOC OQ-4 — Unit 5B medical-vocabulary list.** The 50-word candidate list is assembled from public sources (NABH, Apollo job postings, ESL textbooks) but has not been validated by a working Hyderabad hospital receptionist. Recruit a domain reader (Humanitarians AI volunteer with admin experience, or Apollo / Yashoda / KIMS contact via sisters' network) before Unit 5B publishes.
2. **The Naveena story (Unit 3 opening).** Cannot locate this specific person in public Homes of Hope / Salesian sisters materials. Confirm with Nik / sisters before chapter drafts whether Naveena is a real named graduate (with her permission) or a composite (label as such).
3. **Current 2026 Hyderabad postings.** Real Naukri postings and salary ranges change weekly; the chapter author should pull live samples during drafting for Units 4, 5A/B/C, 7A/B/C.
4. **TIKTOC OQ-2 — Week 3 path-split timing.** Whether the path choice happens at Week 3 (current TIKTOC) or end of Week 1 (so girls can begin track-specific vocabulary from Week 2) is an open design question. The research does not settle it; the sisters' direct knowledge of which girls are interested in which sectors does.
5. **Volunteer training materials (Unit 8).** The mock-interview unit is load-bearing on volunteer-side training. Specific feedback architecture (Hattie & Timperley three-question model, the rehearsal-close protocol) needs a one-page volunteer briefing produced before any Zoom sessions run.
6. **Composite-vignette discipline.** Where Priya is used as the running learner-vignette across Units 1–9, the chapters should label her as a composite (per TIKTOC's learner-profile framing) and not as a real student. Make this clear at first use in the introduction.
7. **The "two biggest first-week failure causes" framing (Unit 9 §A.1).** TIKTOC claims the top two reasons new hires fail in Hyderabad BPO / healthcare first weeks are *not* English. The research note synthesizes this from Bauer & Erdogan's four-factor newcomer-adjustment model + sector-specific commentary, but the specific "top two" claim is operationally defensible rather than empirically established. Either soften to "two of the most consistently reported reasons" or anchor to a specific HR-survey citation.
